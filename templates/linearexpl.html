Linear Search


Explanation


Searching algorithms are used to take a search item (4), and determine whether or not it is present within a list of items (3,2,4,5,1). Linear search is a simple yet highly inefficient searching algorithm. Linear search first takes a search item and a list, and starting from the beginning of the list, compares the search item to each individual item in the list until the item is (or is not) found.
